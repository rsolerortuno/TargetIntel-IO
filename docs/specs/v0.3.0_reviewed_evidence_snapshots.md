# Reviewed-evidence snapshots (v0.3.0)

`create_reviewed_evidence_snapshot` establishes a pure, explicit read boundary
after Issues 306 and 307. It accepts an initialized read-capable EvidenceStore
and immutable request, and never persists, changes, or otherwise mutates
evidence or store metadata. It performs no retrieval, LLM work, scoring,
ranking, synthesis, reporting, or export.

Requests use schema ID `reviewed-evidence-snapshot-request`, version `v1`, a
logical store ID, actor ID, downstream purpose, empty policy, and deterministic
identity. Allowed purposes are `grounded_synthesis`, `scientific_review`,
`controlled_export`, and `reproducibility_check`; they are descriptive only.
Unknown fields, vocabularies, recursive secrets, and hidden reasoning fail
closed.

`explicit_ids` is exact-only, unique, nonblank and canonically ordered.
`field_filter` is exact AND matching over the public scalar EvidenceItem fields
`target_symbol`, `disease_name`, `disease_id`, `evidence_type`, `species`,
`model_system`, `evidence_direction`, `validation_status`, `source`, and
`source_id`. It permits controlled exact-membership values only: no regex,
fuzzy matching, synonym/ontology expansion, predicates, SQL, scoring, or
recency. Field filters require a positive maximum and fail rather than
truncate. An explicit-ID request may also supply a positive maximum; when it
does, the same fail-closed limit is enforced rather than truncating. Empty
snapshots require explicit `allow_empty` and do not imply
negative evidence or absence of evidence.

Items are revalidated with the existing final EvidenceItem validator. The
canonical payload hash is recomputed and checked against the persisted
`record_hash`. Immutable entries retain stable provenance identity references,
not source documents or hidden reasoning. Entries and ID-to-hash manifest pairs
are ordered by EvidenceItem ID and deterministically hashed.

The current EvidenceStore exposes deterministic `list_items` and `get_item`.
It has no transactional manifest/version reads. Construction rereads selected
IDs and hashes before success, detecting observable selected-record changes
without automatic retry; it does not claim full database snapshot isolation.

Snapshots and results exclude operational timestamps, paths, timing, process or
run IDs, logs, and token/provider metadata from deterministic identity.
Snapshots are research-only and non-clinical. Contradictory items remain
separate. Future grounded writers and controlled exports should accept a
`ReviewedEvidenceSnapshot`, not a store, path, raw candidate, claim card, or
unpersisted promotion result. CLI, snapshot-file persistence, and export are
deferred.
