# v0.4.0 — Target feasibility composition

v0.4.0 completes a descriptive, deterministic target-feasibility layer. It is research infrastructure, not biological or clinical validation, a target-superiority claim, a modality recommendation, or a go/no-go system.

Issues 401–405 provide immutable feasibility requests and observations; explicit ranked and directed Open Targets ingestion with query-plan, record/payload, release, cache, resolution, and target-universe identities; explicit coverage rather than confidence; normalized profiles for clinical precedence, modality-specific tractability, doability, safety, missingness, retrieval failure, contradictions, and per-dimension coverage; canonical modality composition; and post-ranking canonical Markdown/HTML decoration.

Directed target universes are independent of association rank. Resolution outcomes, no-record states, and retrieval failures remain visible. Cache and retrieval identities retain source release and verification state. Missing safety data and an empty retrieved safety field do not mean safety; safety wording remains scoped to retrieved source records. Contradictory observations remain visible without averaging, winner selection, or confidence scoring. PROTAC evidence is never inferred from small-molecule evidence.

Feasibility decoration is isolated from deterministic scoring, role classification, feature construction, target selection, ordering, and therapeutic-intent rankings. Existing modality assessments are retained unchanged. No aggregate feasibility score or best modality is generated.

The fully offline [v0.4.0 example](../../examples/feasibility/README.md) composes these public APIs with injected fake transport. It demonstrates ranked-versus-directed retrieval, TAP1/IFNGR1/STAT1 directed handling, coverage reporting, profiles, three modality annotations, canonical cards and HTML, and protected-output invariants. Fixture coverage is not live Open Targets coverage.

Compatibility: this release is additive and post-ranking; existing deterministic pipeline behavior and v0.3.0 grounded evidence/synthesis behavior are unchanged. Validation includes the offline demo regression tests, full repository tests, and deterministic pipeline validation.
